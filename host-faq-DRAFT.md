## 1. Earnings & Revenue Split

### Q: How much can I earn as a mongoori host?
**A:** Most hosts earn $3,000–$5,000 per month, depending on:
- **Your availability** — How many hours/days per month your Tesla is listed
- **Market demand** — Peak seasons (summer, weekends, holidays) earn more
- **Vehicle condition & rating** — Newer, well-maintained vehicles and higher-rated hosts earn premiums
- **Location** — High-demand areas (downtown LA, airport routes, Coastal areas) pay higher rates

**Example:** List your Tesla for 20 days/month at $150/day average = $3,000/month. Full-time availability (25+ days/month) can yield $4,000–$5,000+.

### Q: What's the revenue split between me and mongoori?
**A:** You earn **80% of every ride fare.** mongoori takes 20% to cover:
- Professional insurance (liability, collision, theft)
- Vehicle maintenance & repairs
- 24/7 support team
- Platform operations (app, payment processing, driver vetting)
- Roadside assistance

**Clear breakdown:** If a ride generates $50, you keep $40, mongoori takes $10.

### Q: When do I get paid?
**A:**
- **Weekly automatic deposits** (every Monday) — Most common, zero fees
- **Daily payout option** — Cash out anytime; small fee (2–3%) applies
- **Monthly automatic deposit** — Free transfer on the 1st; great for budgeting

All payments go direct to your bank via ACH. No checks, no delays.

### Q: Are there bonuses for hosts?
**A:** Yes:
- **Referral bonuses** — Earn $200–$500 when you refer another Tesla owner who lists their vehicle
- **Peak-season bonuses** — Higher payouts during high-demand periods (summer, holidays)
- **Rating bonuses** — Hosts with 4.9+ stars get priority for premium rides (higher fares)
- **Loyalty rewards** — Long-term hosts (6+ months) get exclusive opportunities and milestone bonuses

## 2. Insurance & Liability

### Q: Am I liable if a passenger gets injured or property is damaged during a ride?
**A:** No. mongoori carries comprehensive liability insurance covering:
- Passenger injury or illness during rides
- Third-party property damage (other vehicles, buildings, etc.)
- Vehicle damage from passenger misuse

You are **fully protected.** Your personal insurance is never involved.

### Q: Does this affect my personal car insurance?
**A:** No. mongoori's commercial insurance is completely separate from your personal auto policy. Listing your Tesla with mongoori does not:
- Void your personal insurance
- Increase your personal insurance rates
- Require you to notify your personal insurer

**Note:** We recommend informing your personal insurer that your vehicle is used in a commercial rideshare program, but mongoori's insurance is primary.

### Q: What if my Tesla is damaged by a driver or passenger?
**A:** mongoori covers it under our comprehensive liability policy. Here's the process:
1. Report damage via the mongoori app or call support
2. Our team documents the damage and coordinates repairs
3. Repairs are completed at no cost to you
4. You continue earning while your vehicle is being repaired (we provide a loaner or temporarily pause your listing)

**Deductible:** $0 for normal wear & tear, $250 deductible for passenger-caused damage (accident, spill, etc.). Most hosts never hit the deductible.

### Q: What about theft or total loss?
**A:** Your Tesla is fully insured against theft and total loss. If your vehicle is stolen or totaled:
- mongoori's insurance covers the vehicle's full replacement value
- We work directly with insurers to get you paid quickly
- You're not out a penny

## 3. Vehicle Management & Wear & Tear

### Q: How many miles/km can my Tesla accumulate before I owe mongoori?
**A:** No mileage cap. Your Tesla can accumulate unlimited miles while listed with mongoori. However:
- **Expected wear & tear** is normal and covered by mongoori
- **Maintenance & repairs** (battery, motors, brakes) are 100% covered by mongoori
- You never pay for routine or major repairs

The only exception: If you cause **intentional or negligent damage** (e.g., crash due to recklessness, vandalism), mongoori may pursue recovery.

### Q: Who pays for maintenance (oil changes, tire rotations, repairs)?
**A:** mongoori pays for **everything.** This includes:
- Scheduled maintenance (battery checks, software updates)
- Tire rotations & replacements
- Brake service
- Body repairs (dents, scratches from passenger use)
- Battery diagnostics & replacement (if needed)

You never pay a dime for maintenance or repairs while your Tesla is listed.

### Q: What if my Tesla breaks down while a driver has it?
**A:** mongoori covers it completely:
1. Driver contacts support
2. Roadside assistance or towing arrives (typically 15–30 min)
3. Repairs happen at mongoori's cost
4. Vehicle is returned to you or replaced with a loaner
5. You continue earning while repairs are made (optional: pause your listing)

### Q: Can I use my Tesla myself while it's listed?
**A:** Yes. You can:
- Drive it personally whenever you want
- Mark yourself as unavailable in the mongoori app (optional: block out dates/times)
- Switch between "available for rides" and "personal use" anytime

However, once a driver books your Tesla, they have exclusive use until the ride/rental period ends.

### Q: What if I want to stop hosting temporarily?
**A:** Easy. Just pause your listing in the mongoori app. You can:
- Pause for a few days (vacation, personal use)
- Pause for a month or longer
- Resume anytime—no penalties, no fees

**A:** Any Tesla model in good condition:
- **Accepted models:** Model 3, Model Y, Model S, Model X
- **Year requirement:** 2018 or newer (good condition)
- **Condition requirement:** Interior & exterior in good, clean condition; no major accidents

**A:** We can still list it if:
- **Minor damage** — Repaired to original condition (cosmetic fixes, small dents)

- **Flood/fire damage** — Cannot be listed; safety & structural concerns

Be transparent during the application. mongoori's assessment determines eligibility.

- Commercial plates or registration
- Special permits
- Changes to your vehicle registration

### Q: What's the eligibility criteria for hosts?
**A:**
- **Age:** 25+
- **Valid CA driver's license**
- **Vehicle:** Tesla model 2018+, in good condition
- **Clean driving record:** No serious violations (DUIs, reckless driving)
- **Insurance requirement:** Personal insurance must be valid (mongoori's commercial coverage is primary)

### Q: Do I need to pass any inspections?

## 7. Taxes & Deductions

### Q: Do I report hosting income on my taxes?
**A:** Yes. mongoori hosting income is taxable:
- **You're self-employed** — Hosting income is treated as self-employment income
- **Federal tax** — You're responsible for federal income tax (rate depends on your bracket)
- **California state tax** — California income tax applies
- **Self-employment tax** — You owe self-employment tax (~15.3% on net income)

**Rate estimate:** Set aside 25–30% of earnings for federal, state, and SE tax.

### Q: Does mongoori provide tax documents?
**A:** Yes. At year-end, we provide:
- **1099-NEC or 1099-MISC** — Shows total hosting income (for IRS filing)
- **Detailed earnings report** — Monthly breakdown by ride, tips, bonuses
- **Deduction summary** — Vehicle-related expenses covered by mongoori (for records)

These documents are ready by Jan 31 for tax filing.

### Q: What deductions can I claim?
**A:** You can deduct:
- **Vehicle depreciation** — Based on vehicle value and age
- **Insurance** — Your personal auto insurance premiums (mongoori's commercial coverage is separate)
- **Maintenance** — Any personal maintenance NOT covered by mongoori (e.g., after-market upgrades)
- **Phone & app** — Portion of your phone bill used for mongoori business
- **Home office** — If you have a dedicated space for recordkeeping

**Note:** Consult a tax professional to maximize deductions and ensure compliance.

### Q: Does mongoori hosting affect my personal taxes or benefits?
**A:** Potentially yes:
- **Self-employment tax** — You'll owe additional SE tax
- **Income limits** — May affect eligibility for certain government benefits (if applicable)
- **Loan/credit applications** — Lenders may count mongoori income as business income (favorable)

Consult a tax professional or financial advisor for your specific situation.
