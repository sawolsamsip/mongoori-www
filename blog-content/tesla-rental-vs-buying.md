## Breaking Down Ownership Costs

Buying a Tesla involves multiple expense categories:

**Down Payment and Financing:** Most buyers finance through banks, Tesla's own program, or dealer financing. A typical down payment is 10-20% ($4,200-$11,600), with loan payments extending 60-72 months. Monthly payments typically range from $600-$900, totaling $43,200-$64,800 over six years.

**Insurance:** Commercial rideshare insurance is mandatory and costs significantly more than personal auto insurance. Expect $2,000-$3,500 annually for commercial coverage—roughly $40-$67 per week.

**Maintenance and Repairs:** Teslas are famous for low maintenance. No oil changes, no transmission fluid, minimal brake service due to regenerative braking. However, battery replacements (rare but possible) cost $8,000-$12,000. Tire replacements run $250-$400 per tire. Most owner experience $500-$1,200 in annual maintenance.

**Registration and Taxes:** California vehicle registration averages $250-$300 annually. If purchasing through a dealership, add sales tax (varies by county, typically 7.25%-8.5%).

**Depreciation:** Teslas hold value better than many vehicles, but depreciation is real. A $50,000 Model 3 might be worth $35,000 after 4 years—a $15,000 loss.

**Total six-year ownership cost:** $48,200-$67,300 (down payment, financing, insurance, maintenance, registration, depreciation, taxes).
