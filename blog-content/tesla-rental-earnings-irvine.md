## Pricing Strategy for Tesla Rental Drivers

Here's the uncomfortable truth: your Tesla rental vehicle is a premium product competing against standard rideshare vehicles. You must price accordingly.

**Leverage Premium Positioning**: Tesla rental in Irvine allows you to position as a premium service. Your vehicle is newer, cleaner, more comfortable, and technologically advanced than 95% of competing rideshare vehicles. This justifies:
- Accepting only higher-rated riders (4.8+ stars)
- Declining low-value trips ($5-8 short rides)
- Positioning for premium trip types (airport, business, special occasions)

**Selective Trip Acceptance**: This is controversial but statistically proven. Tesla rental drivers who accept 60% of trip requests generate more earnings than those accepting 85%+. Why? Selection bias. By declining low-value trips, you remain available for high-value opportunities that appear in the next 2-3 minutes.

A $6 trip that takes 12 minutes represents $30/hour earning potential. But if that trip prevents you from accepting a $28 airport transfer that appears 3 minutes later, you've lost $140 in opportunity cost.

**Dynamic Positioning**: Rather than accepting any trip from anywhere, strategic Tesla rental drivers position themselves to maximize higher-value trip capture:
- Morning: Near major office parks and business hotels
- Noon-3pm: Near shopping/dining centers and medical facilities
- Evening: Near office parks, airports approach routes
- Night: Entertainment districts

This positioning strategy creates opportunity asymmetry where high-value trips find you, rather than forcing you to hunt.
