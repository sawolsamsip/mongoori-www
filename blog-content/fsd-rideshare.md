## What is Tesla Full Self-Driving?

FSD is an advanced driver assistance system (ADAS)—not true autonomous driving. Here's the technical distinction:

**What FSD Can Do:**
- Navigate highways with adaptive cruise control and lane-keeping
- Change lanes automatically (with driver confirmation)
- Navigate city streets with traffic light and stop sign recognition
- Park automatically (parallel and perpendicular)
- Summon the car from a parking spot
- Route intelligently based on traffic conditions
- Handle congested traffic with semi-autonomous "creep" mode

**What FSD Cannot Do:**
- Drive without a driver behind the wheel and attentive
- Drop passengers and drive away
- Handle all weather conditions reliably (rain, snow reduce capability)
- Replace driver attention during complex intersections
- Operate in all regions (limited in some rural areas, poor infrastructure zones)

The crucial point: **FSD requires active driver supervision.** You must stay alert, hands ready on the wheel, eyes on the road. It reduces effort and fatigue but doesn't eliminate driver responsibility.

## Why Fatigue Matters for Rideshare Profitability

Rideshare driving is exhausting. A typical full-time driver logs 250-400 miles per week, navigating traffic, managing passenger interactions, and dealing with navigation stress.

**The fatigue problem:**
- After 8+ hours of driving, reaction times slow by 30-50%
- Decision-making becomes impaired (equivalent to moderate alcohol consumption)
- Risk of accidents increases dramatically
- Passenger satisfaction drops (passengers sense driver stress)
- Driving quality suffers, reducing Uber/Lyft ratings

Lower ratings trigger:
- Fewer premium ride requests (Uber Comfort, Black)
- Reduced acceptance of surge pricing opportunities
- Potential deactivation if rating drops below 4.6

FSD doesn't eliminate fatigue, but it significantly reduces the active mental and physical effort during highway driving and congested city streets.

## FSD Limitations You Should Know

**Geographic constraints:** FSD maps certain regions in California extremely well (Bay Area, LA metro, San Diego). Rural areas, small cities, and newly developed zones may lack FSD's detailed map data.

**Weather sensitivity:** Rain, fog, and snow significantly reduce FSD's capability. California's relatively stable weather is an advantage here, but expect FSD to disengage in heavy rain or unusual conditions.

**Construction zones:** FSD struggles with active construction, lane closures, and detour routing. You'll need to take manual control.

**Passenger dynamics:** FSD's predictable acceleration and braking sometimes concern passengers unfamiliar with the technology. Be prepared to explain: "This is Tesla's autopilot managing the route—I'm monitoring everything."
