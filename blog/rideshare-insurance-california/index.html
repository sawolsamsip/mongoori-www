How to Qualify for Rideshare Insurance in California

March 16, 2026  ·  6 min read

If you drive for Uber or Lyft in California, you face a risk that most new drivers don't discover until it's too late: a dangerous insurance coverage gap. Your personal auto insurance doesn't cover you while the app is open, and Uber/Lyft's commercial coverage doesn't kick in fully until you accept a ride. Understanding California's rideshare insurance requirements — and how to close that gap — is essential before you hit the road.

The California Rideshare Insurance Gap Explained

California law (AB 2293) requires rideshare companies to provide commercial insurance, but the coverage level depends on which "period" of the ride you're in:

  • Period 0 — App off: Only your personal auto insurance applies.
  • Period 1 — App on, waiting for a match: Uber/Lyft provides limited liability ($50K per person, $100K per accident, $25K property). Your personal policy does NOT apply.
  • Period 2 — Accepted a ride, en route to pickup: Full commercial coverage ($1M liability) kicks in.
  • Period 3 — Passenger in car: Full $1M commercial coverage continues.
Period 1 is the danger zone. You're working, but only minimally covered — and most personal policies will deny claims if you had the app open.

What California Requires for Rideshare Drivers

California requires all rideshare drivers to maintain valid auto insurance at all times. Specifically:

  • Minimum liability: 15/30/5 (California minimums — though higher limits are strongly recommended)
  • Your personal insurer must be notified you drive rideshare, or your policy may be voided
  • You must carry proof of insurance both during personal driving and rideshare periods

Many California insurers will cancel your policy if they discover you drive TNC (Transportation Network Company) vehicles without disclosure. Always disclose rideshare use when applying for or renewing insurance.

California Carriers That Offer Rideshare Insurance

The following insurers offer rideshare endorsements or policies in California (as of 2026):

  • Mercury Insurance — Rideshare endorsement available; popular in Southern California
  • USAA — Rideshare coverage for military families
  • Farmers — TNC endorsement that fills the Period 1 gap
  • Allstate — Ride for Hire endorsement; affordable add-on
  • Progressive — Rideshare coverage available; wide California network

Typical cost for a rideshare endorsement: $10–$25/month added to your existing premium. A small price for full peace of mind across all three periods.

What to Ask Your Insurer

  • "Does my policy cover me while the rideshare app is open but I haven't accepted a ride yet?"
  • "Do you offer a TNC/rideshare endorsement in California?"
  • "Will you cancel my policy if I disclose rideshare driving?"

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need rideshare insurance if I own my own car in California?

Yes. Your personal policy likely excludes coverage when the rideshare app is open. You need either a rideshare endorsement from your insurer or a separate commercial policy to close the Period 1 gap.

What happens if I get in an accident in Period 1 without rideshare coverage?

Your personal insurer can deny the claim entirely. Uber/Lyft's limited liability may not cover all damages, leaving you personally liable for the difference.

Is rideshare insurance required by California law?

California requires TNC companies to maintain commercial coverage for drivers. However, drivers are not explicitly required by law to carry separate rideshare endorsements on personal policies — but failing to do so creates significant financial exposure.
