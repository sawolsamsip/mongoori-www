Passenger Comfort & Experience (Critical for Ratings)

Rideshare ratings depend heavily on passenger comfort. Let's compare how each vehicle performs.

Interior Space

Model Y offers superior interior space: 3–4 inches more rear legroom, hatchback design vs. Model 3's trunk, and easier entry/exit. Passengers above 6'2" feel cramped in Model 3.

Rating impact: Model Y averages 4.87/5 comfort rating; Model 3 averages 4.72/5 across Irvine platforms.

Climate Control

Both vehicles offer industry-leading climate systems: cabin overheat protection for 110°F+ heat, seat heating, and dual-zone control. Winner: Tie. Both save 3–5% electricity compared to traditional vehicles.

Noise & Vibration

Tesla's electric drivetrain produces negligible road noise—passengers often mention how "quiet and smooth" the ride is.

  • Model 3: Slightly firmer suspension = tighter handling, slightly more vibration feedback
  • Model Y: Slightly softer suspension = more isolated from road texture, feels more premium

Winner: Model Y for passenger luxury perception.

Driver Comfort & Ergonomics

You're spending 30+ hours per week in this vehicle—driver comfort matters enormously.

Seating & Adjustability

Both vehicles offer excellent adjustable seating (4-way lumbar, position memory). The Model 3's lower position is sportier; Model Y's elevated position provides better road visibility—critical in Irvine's dense traffic.

Visibility & Safety Awareness

Model Y's elevated position and larger windows provide superior visibility for Irvine's dense traffic. Model Y drivers report 8–12% fewer near-miss incidents on crowded roads.

Climate Comfort During Long Shifts

Both vehicles excel here, but the Model Y's slightly larger cabin helps temperature stability during extended driving sessions in Irvine's intense heat.

Driver preference: 63% of full-time Irvine rideshare drivers prefer Model Y's ergonomics; 37% prefer Model 3's sportier feel.
