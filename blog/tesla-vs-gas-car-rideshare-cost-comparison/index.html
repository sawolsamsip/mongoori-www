Detailed Cost Analysis: Where Tesla Wins

1. Fuel Costs: The Biggest Savings

Gas vehicle fuel costs:

  • Your Toyota Camry gets 25 MPG combined (EPA rating for 2024 model)
  • Irvine rideshare drivers average 1,200 miles/month
  • At $3.50/gallon (2026 CA average), you spend $168/month on gas
  • Annual gas cost: $2,016

Tesla fuel costs (electricity):

  • Tesla Model Y gets 4.0 miles/kWh (EPA equivalent)
  • Same 1,200 miles/month
  • At $0.12/kWh (CA rate), you spend $36/month charging
  • Annual electricity cost: $432
  • Savings: $1,584/year

Why the difference?

  • Electricity is 70% cheaper than gasoline on a per-mile basis
  • Tesla's efficiency (4.0 mi/kWh) equals ~120 MPG equivalent
  • Most Irvine home chargers use nighttime rates ($0.08–0.10/kWh)
  • Mobile/fast charging available at superchargers

2. Maintenance: Minimal for Tesla

Gas vehicle maintenance:

  • Oil changes: $65 × 4 per year = $260
  • Tire rotation/replacement: $150/year (more frequent, 25k–40k miles)
  • Brake service: $500 annually
  • Repairs & unexpected issues: $1,000–2,000
  • Transmission fluid, coolant, spark plugs, filters
  • Annual maintenance: $1,500–2,500

Tesla maintenance:

  • Brake fluid only (non-corrosive): $50/year
  • Tire rotation included in rental: $0
  • Regenerative braking reduces brake wear to 10% of gas vehicles
  • No oil, transmission, spark plugs, or filters
  • mongoori covers unexpected repairs in rental
  • Annual maintenance: $150–300
  • Savings: $1,350/year

4. Vehicle Cost: Rental vs. Ownership Flexibility

Ownership costs are fixed. You pay $1,000/month whether you earn $500 or $5,000 that month.

Rental costs are flexible. Take 5 days? Pay $325. Take 30 days? Pay $1,950. No long-term commitment.

For drivers with variable schedules, Tesla rental is far superior to vehicle ownership.
