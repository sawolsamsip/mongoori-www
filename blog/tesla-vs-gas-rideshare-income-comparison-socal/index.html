This comprehensive analysis compares real financial data to help you make the right vehicle decision for your rideshare business.

Break-Even Analysis: When Does Tesla Become Cheaper?

Let's calculate the exact break-even point where a Tesla's total cost advantage exceeds its higher upfront cost.

Assumptions:

  • Tesla initial cost: $42,990
  • Camry initial cost: $28,500
  • Monthly cost advantage (Tesla): $1,420 - $720 = $700/month
  • Annual earnings advantage (Tesla): $6,120/year

Monthly advantage (cost + earnings):

  • Operating cost savings: $700/month
  • Earnings bonus: $510/month ($6,120 ÷ 12)
  • Total monthly advantage: $1,210

Break-even calculation:

  • Initial price difference: $14,490
  • Monthly advantage: $1,210
  • Break-even period: $14,490 ÷ $1,210 = 12 months (1 year)

Conclusion: A Tesla becomes financially superior to a gas vehicle within the first 12-18 months of rideshare operation, accounting for both operating cost savings and higher earnings potential.
